# Research Discussion Playbook

A research interview tests judgment, not merely whether you can recite a paper.

## 90-second project structure

1. **Problem:** What decision or scientific gap mattered?
2. **Insight:** What hypothesis made your approach plausible?
3. **Method:** Data, model, baseline, validation, and ablations.
4. **Result:** Main measured result plus uncertainty/limitations.
5. **Judgment:** What failed, what you learned, and what you would do next.

## Your preparation map

Prepare public, non-confidential versions of:

- Medicine4Youth: clinical ML prediction, validation, interpretability, responsible use.
- Stanford AI in Healthcare: Neural ODEs, irregular clinical time series, MIMIC-style data.
- UT research: archival/textual data, OCR/metadata/LLM workflows, reliability and ethics.
- Frigate / systems work: model impact, data quality, monitoring, latency/cost trade-offs.
- Amazon internship: only a high-level, non-confidential description of the problem space and the engineering skills you are learning; never disclose internal architecture, data, metrics, or roadmap.
- Quant work: leakage-safe validation, execution realism, drawdown, monitoring, and assumptions.

## Follow-up checklist

Be ready for: baseline choice, ablation, failure cases, data leakage, confounders, statistical uncertainty, reproducibility, ethical risk, deployment impact, and next experiment.
