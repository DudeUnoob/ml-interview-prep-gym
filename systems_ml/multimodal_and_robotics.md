# Multimodal and Robotics / Perception Prompts

## Concepts to explain

- Early fusion versus late fusion
- Contrastive pretraining versus generative multimodal modeling
- Camera calibration, coordinate frames, and temporal alignment
- Dataset shifts: weather, geography, sensors, long-tail events
- Closed-loop versus open-loop evaluation for planning
- Safety envelope, uncertainty, and fallback behavior

## Design prompts

1. Design a vision-language system that finds safety-relevant events in long driving clips.
2. A model improves offline detection mAP but makes downstream planning worse. What could explain that?
3. Build a data engine for rare-event discovery and targeted labeling.
4. You have limited GPU budget: choose architecture, training objective, and evaluation plan for a perception model.

## Your framing

For roles where you lack direct autonomous-driving production experience: state that gap plainly, then bridge through ML rigor, systems experience, research habits, and willingness to work in C++/performance-sensitive code. Do not claim proprietary or unavailable experience.
