# 60-Minute Mock Interview Protocol

## 0–5 min: warm-up

- Tell me about yourself for this role.
- Why this team / why now?

## 5–25 min: coding

Use one DSA or ML-coding prompt. Speak through clarification, brute force, invariant, implementation, tests, and complexity.

## 25–40 min: technical discussion

Choose one: attention/debugging, LLM inference, distributed training, evaluation, multimodal system, or experiment design.

## 40–52 min: research discussion

Give one 90-second project pitch. Expect follow-ups on baselines, ablations, data leakage, limitations, and next steps.

## 52–58 min: behavioral

One STAR prompt plus one follow-up.

## 58–60 min: questions

Ask two questions about team problems, success metrics, technical decisions, or collaboration. Avoid questions already answered publicly.

## Scorecard (1–5 each)

- Problem framing
- Technical correctness
- Communication
- Code quality / test discipline
- Trade-off reasoning
- Depth of research judgment
- Ownership and reflection

Log the score and a single highest-leverage fix in `notes/interview_log.md`.
